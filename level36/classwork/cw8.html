In 1999, Japan saw a new version of the Silvia, the S15, now producing 250 PS (247 bhp; 184 kW) at 6,400 rpm and 275 N⋅m; 203 lbf⋅ft (28 kg⋅m) of torque at 4,800 rpm from its SR20DET inline-four engine, [33] thanks to a ball bearing turbocharger upgrade, as well as an improved engine management system. on-turbo SR20DE produces 165 PS (163 bhp; 121 kW).[34] This generation of the Silvia was only sold new in Japan, Australia and New Zealand, with the latter two being sold as the Nissan 200SX with various differences between the two markets.
